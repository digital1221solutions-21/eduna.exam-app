3rd Grade Curriculum Practice

Based on Glen Burnie, Maryland Standards

Level 1: Basic Addition (2-digit)

Let's start with the basics! In this level, you'll learn how to add two-digit numbers (like 25 + 14) where you don't have to 'carry over' any numbers. It's the first step to becoming a math whiz!

Level 2: Basic Subtraction (2-digit)

Time to practice taking away! This level covers subtracting two-digit numbers (like 48 - 23) without needing to 'borrow'. This helps you get fast and accurate at subtraction.

Level 3: Addition with Regrouping

This is where it gets interesting! You'll learn how to "carry over" or "regroup" when your columns add up to more than 9 (like 37 + 45). This is a super important skill for all future math.

Level 4: Subtraction with Regrouping

Ready for a challenge? This level teaches "borrowing" for subtraction. You'll learn what to do when the top number is smaller than the bottom number (like 52 - 17). Master this, and you can subtract anything!

Level 5: Intro to Multiplication (0-5)

Multiplication is just fast adding! Here you'll learn what 3 x 4 means (three groups of four) and master your first times tables: the 0s, 1s, 2s, and 5s.

Level 6: Multiplication Facts (6-10)

Let's complete your times tables! This level focuses on the trickier numbers: 6, 7, 8, 9, and 10. We'll learn tricks to remember them and practice until they're automatic.

Level 7: Intro to Division

Division is all about sharing equally. You'll learn how division is the opposite of multiplication (if 3 x 4 = 12, then 12 ÷ 3 = 4) and practice your first division problems.

Level 8: Understanding Fractions

What is 1/2? Or 1/3? This level introduces you to the world of fractions! You'll learn how to read, write, and understand parts of a whole, like sharing a pizza with friends.

Level 9: Telling Time & Money

Let's use math in the real world! You'll practice telling time to the nearest minute on a clock and learn how to count and make change with coins (pennies, nickels, dimes, quarters) and bills.

Level 10: Mixed Review Word Problems

Put it all together! This final math level gives you tricky word problems where you have to decide if you need to add, subtract, multiply, or divide. You'll become a math detective!

Level 1: Spelling Basics

Let's become amazing spellers! This level helps you practice common 3rd-grade words and learn basic spelling rules, like when to use 'ie' or 'ei'.

Level 2: Vocabulary Building

Words are power! You'll learn new, exciting words, find out what they mean (their definitions), and practice using them in your own sentences to sound like a genius.

Level 3: Identifying Nouns

What is a noun? It's a person (like 'teacher'), a place (like 'school'), or a thing (like 'book'). You'll become an expert at finding them in sentences!

Level 4: Identifying Verbs

Verbs are action words! You'll learn to spot words that show action (like 'run', 'jump', 'think') and "helping" verbs (like 'is', 'are', 'was') that connect sentences.

Level 5: Adjectives and Adverbs

Let's add some detail! Adjectives describe nouns (like 'happy dog'), and adverbs describe verbs (like 'run quickly'). You'll learn to use them to make your writing more colorful.

Level 6: Punctuation (Periods, Commas)

Punctuation helps us know when to stop and pause. You'll master using periods (.), question marks (?), and exclamation points (!) at the end of sentences, and commas (,) in lists.

Level 7: Capitalization

When do you use a big letter? This level teaches all the important rules, like capitalizing the first word of a sentence, names of people ('John'), places ('Maryland'), and holidays ('Christmas').

Level 8: Reading Comprehension 1

Let's read a story! After reading a short passage, you'll practice answering questions to find the "main idea" (what the story is mostly about) and find important "key details" from the text.

Level 9: Reading Comprehension 2

Time to read like a detective! You'll read slightly longer stories and learn to "make inferences" (figure out things the story doesn't tell you directly) and understand a character's feelings.

Level 10: Writing Prompts

Now it's your turn to be the author! This level gives you fun ideas (prompts) to write about, helping you practice writing complete sentences and organizing your thoughts into a short paragraph.

Level 1: Living and Non-Living

What's the difference between a rock and a puppy? In this level, you'll learn the characteristics of living things (like breathing, growing, and eating) and how to sort things into "living" and "non-living" groups.

Level 2: Plant Life Cycle

How does a tiny seed become a big flower? You'll learn all about the amazing life cycle of a plant, from a seed to a sprout, to a full-grown plant with flowers and fruits.

Level 3: Animal Life Cycles

Animals grow and change, too! You'll explore the life cycles of different animals, like how a tadpole turns into a frog, or how a caterpillar becomes a beautiful butterfly (metamorphosis).

Level 4: Animal Habitats

Every animal has a home! You'll learn about different "habitats" or homes (like the hot desert, the cold arctic, the wet ocean, and the busy forest) and which animals are special to each one.

Level 5: Food Chains

Where does energy come from? You'll learn how energy moves from the sun to "producers" (plants), to "consumers" (animals that eat plants or other animals) in a simple food chain.

Level 6: The Solar System

Let's go to space! You'll learn the names of the planets in our solar system (like Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) and learn amazing facts about our Sun and Moon.

Level 7: Earth's Rotation (Day/Night)

Why does the sun "go down" at night? You'll learn that the Earth is always spinning (rotating) on its axis, and this spin is what causes the cycle of day and night.

Level 8: Weather and Seasons

Why is it hot in summer and cold in winter? You'll learn about the four seasons (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter) and how the Earth's tilt causes them. You'll also learn to measure weather with a thermometer.

Level 9: States of Matter

Everything is made of matter! You'll learn to identify the three states of matter: "solid" (like an ice cube), "liquid" (like water), and "gas" (like steam), and how they can change.

Level 10: Simple Machines

How do we make work easier? By using simple machines! You'll learn to identify and understand levers (like a seesaw), pulleys, and inclined planes (like a ramp).
